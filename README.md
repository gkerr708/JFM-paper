### Cover Letter
Dear Professor Morris,

Please find enclosed our manuscript entitled Liquid Bridge Stabilization by Surface Skin Formation Enables Contact Drawing of Protein Microfibres by Gavin A. Kerr, Grace O'Connor, Grecia Valeria Sanchez Ruiz, Hessam Yaghoobi Koopaee, and Laurent Kreplak, which we submit for consideration as a full article in the Journal of Fluid Mechanics. In this work, we investigate the stabilization of liquid bridges during contact drawing of protein solutions. Fiber formation from liquid bridges has previously been demonstrated for high molecular weight polymer solutions, where long viscoelastic relaxation times suppress capillary thinning. In contrast, the proteins studied here have significantly lower molecular weight and therefore cannot sustain comparable bulk viscoelastic stresses. Despite this, we find that stable liquid bridges can still be formed and drawn into long fibers. We demonstrate that rapid surface skin formation provides an alternative mechanism for stabilization. The rapidly forming skin behaves as a viscoelastic shell that resists capillary thinning and stabilizes the liquid bridge during extension. Using high-speed imaging of the cone region, we examine how protein concentration, drawing time, and solvent volatility influence the evolution and stability of the bridge. We believe this work will be of interest to readers of Journal of Fluid Mechanics because it identifies a distinct mechanism for liquid bridge stabilization and connects extensional flow dynamics with evaporation-driven skin formation. Thank you for considering our manuscript.
